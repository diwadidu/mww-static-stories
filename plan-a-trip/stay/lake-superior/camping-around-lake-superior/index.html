Camping around Lake Superior

dd

No summer vacation is more fun than a Circle Tour of one of the Great Lakes � and nothing is more of a pain than planning one.

Fans of lighthouses and dramatic cliffs love Lake Superior, which is lined by state and city parks with gorgeous stretches of cobblestone beach and forest. You can�t buy a better vacation at any price, but you have to plan ahead.

Planning is tricky because you pass through three states, a Canadian province and one very popular national lakeshores.

You have to navigate around big festivals that fill every hotel and campground within 50 miles � if you arrive in Grand Marais without a reservation during Fisherman's Picnic, or in Duluth any weekend, and you�ll be sleeping in your car.

Nearly every desirable place in tourist areas has a two-night minimum on weekends, so you�ll either be spending two nights or staying in a Super 8.

ADS:4

And you can�t count on getting walk-up campsites: In Michigan, the most popular beach parks are 100 percent reservable, and weekends are booked immediately when reservations open six months in advance.

If you want to camp indoors on a summer weekend in Michigan state parks, it's best to reserve the mini-cabins, rustic cabins, camper cabins and guesthouses along this Midwestern Riviera a year in advance.

But midweek openings stay available much longer, especially in state parks on the Upper Peninsula.

For last-minute stays, look for parks with rustic or hike-in sites, which are slower to be reserved. For example, Crosby-Manitou State Park, in the hills between Silver Bay and Tofte on Minnesota's North Shore, has plenty of sites not far from Lake Superior.
