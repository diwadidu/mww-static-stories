How to make a snowman

ddd

In what has become an annual tradition of this newsletter, here is the always popular step-by-step guide for building your own historic snowman.

Snowmen are considered a symbol of the winter season for many, often appearing on holiday cards and in front yards across Wisconsin. According to old diaries and chronicles, snowman construction dates back to at least the Middle Ages. With winter in full force, it is our pleasure to provide detailed instuctions for making your very own historic snowman.

Step 1 Make sure there is at least a few inches of snow on the ground and you have 15-30 minutes of free time. Don't forget to wear gloves or mittens as you will be handling snow. Test to see if you have "packing snow"; snow that clumps together easily and isn't too wet. The snow must pack to make a snowman.

Step 2 Shape a handful of snow into a ball. Do not give in to temptation and throw the snowball at the neighbor kids. Continue adding more snow and packing the ball until it's too large to hold.

ADS:4

Step 3 Place the large snowball on the snow in front of you and slowly roll it away from you. As your snowball accumulates more snow and becomes larger, pack the snow by pressing on it with your gloved hands.

Step 4 Keep rolling and packing the ball until it is the size you want for the bottom of the snowman's body (typically, the size of a small boulder).

Step 5 Repeat steps 2-4 to create the midsection and the snowman's head. The bottom should be the biggest ball, and the top should be the smallest (about the size of an inflated beach ball).

Step 6 Pack some extra snow between the layers to make them stick together. Place sticks within the center where the sections meet if your snowman is having trouble standing. However, in some counties, this may be considered "cheating."

Step 7 The most important step is making the snowman's face. Use coal, rocks, buttons or anything dark and round for the eyes. A horizontal stick or twig will make a good mouth, and a true snowman will always have a carrot for its nose.

Step 8 Add arms, legs and other accessories. Push sticks into the sides of the middle section and hang old mittens on the ends. Place boots at the bottom for legs. Also consider adding items such as a shirt, a scarf or sunglasses. Cover the top of his head with an old plant for hair, or give him a knit hat to wear. Top hats tend to blow away on windy days unless secured.

Step 9 Take lots of photos of your new friend. Finally, no matter how dirty it may get, never give your snowman a hot bath.
