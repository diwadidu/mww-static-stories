Best things to do and see in Houghton, Michigan

Houghton

From 2019: Take a Keweenaw Waterway Cruise on the Ranger III Ferry ! Back due to popular demand, the Thursday daytime cruises depart at 12:30 pm from the Houghton Visitor's Center and last approximately 3 hours. Your trip will take you south along the Keweenaw Waterway to the South Entry of Keweenaw Bay before returning to Houghton. Refreshments will be available. Don't wait to get your tickets though, the July 18th cruise is almost sold out. There is availability on the July 11th, August 8th and August 29th trips. Call the Houghton Visitor's Center for more info: 906-482-0984

Accommodations: the new Vault hotel. Copper Country Inn, mom and pop motel on east side of town.

Dining: Ambassador Restaurant. East of Hancock on Dollar Bay, Quincy's Restaurant, atmospheric.

From: http://www.smithsonianmag.com/travel/20-best-small-towns-visit-2016-180958799/

"Houghton might be a four-season destination for tourists looking for an off-the-beaten-path outdoor escape, but hockey takes center stage all-year-long for locals.

Houghton considers itself the birthplace of professional hockey, something that its Canadian neighbors to the north might take umbrage with. But its claim has legitimacy, as Houghton was home to the world�s first pro hockey league, founded there in 1904. (The first organized indoor game of hockey, however, was played ​between amateurs at Montreal's Victoria Skating Rink in 1875.) Though the original rink, "The Dee,� constructed in 1902, burned in a fire in the 1920s, the town quickly rebuilt the rink, which is still operational today.

Houghton sits atop one of the world�s largest deposit of native copper, and for years the town�s economy depended on local mines, which produced more than 10 billion pounds of refined copper, before the city�s economy began to shift toward tourism, its primary focus today. The area's natural resources can still be seen on view at the A. E. Seaman Mineral Museum.

Part of the city�s appeal is its remote location, as it takes four hours by car to get there from the closest major city. The entryway to the Keweenaw peninsula, visitors enjoy snowmobiling and skiing in the winter in the town, while boating, hiking and biking in summer months proves equally popular. The Isle Royale National Park is less than 50 miles away, providing an even larger playground for those in search of adventure.

Before leaving Houghton City though, play a trip to The Ambassador. A delicious eatery for grabbing a slice of pizza, homemade from recipes passed down for decades, the historic space itself is a treat to look at with murals almost a century old.

(from Michigan's Keweenaw Peninsula Facebook page)

Did you know Houghton is the "Birthplace of Professional Hockey"?

117 years ago the Copper Country was buzzing with excitement and anticipation of the "World Championship Game" between the "Canadian Champions", the Montreal Wanderers and the Keweenaw's very own Portage Lakes Hockey Club.

The Montreal Wanderers had made the 900 mile trip to battle it out with the best of the U.S....

There would be two games. Tickets were $1.00-$2.00 and had sold out within an hour of going on sale. Trains were scheduled to bring in visitors from as far away as 500 miles.

When the puck dropped there were around 3,000 people inside the Amphidrome, and an additional 1,000-1,500 outside the arena. Portage handily took both games in front of a rapturous crowd that carried the champions off the ice.

The 1903-04 Portage Lakes Hockey Club were a phenomenal team. They had an exhilarating combination of speed, skill, and aggression. They ended the season with a jaw-dropping 258 goals to their opponents 49!
