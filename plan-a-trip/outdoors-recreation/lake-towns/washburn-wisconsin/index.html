Washburn

Washburn

Trip Tips: Washburn in northwest Wisconsin

Getting there: It's 12 miles from Bayfield and also Ashland on Wisconsin 13.

2016 events: May, Chequamegon Nature Fest. July, Brownstone Summerfest. October, Zombie Days.

On President's Day weekend in February, Book Across the Bay, a 10-kilometer race or tour across Chequamegon Bay from Ashland to Washburn, a benefit for the Washburn Public Library.

Accommodations: The North Coast Inn is a mom-and-pop motel that includes chalets with kitchenettes, 715-373-5512.

The McGrath House is in town near the beach and has two bedrooms, two baths and sleeps eight, 715-373-5428.

The Inn at Washburn, formerly a Super 8, is right on the waterfront in town, 715-373-5671.

Three miles north of town, Antique Garden Inn B&B rents two rooms, a cottage and a loft.

The Bayfield area has many other inns, condos and motels; see Bayfield in winter.

North of town, Bodin's Resort is on Chequamegon Bay and rents cabins from May through October.

Camping: Two municipal campgrounds along the lake have showers, a sand beach, a playground and wifi. They're walking distance to shops and restaurants.

Memorial Park and Campground has many pines and is best suited for small RVs and tents. More than half of its sites can be reserved.

Thompson's West End Park and Campground has a boat launch, fishing pier and historic artesian well. It's first come, first served.

Dining: Good Thyme Restaurant occupies the 1913 Monroe Sprague house, a mile north on Wisconsin 13. Call 715-373-5255 for reservations.

Cafe Coco serves breakfast and lunch and is a good place to pick up sandwiches and baked goods.

Patsy's Bar & Grill, where Warren Nelson & friends recorded two live CDs, is a good place to get a burger. It was recently renovated and has an outdoor eating area.

DaLou's Bistro offers wood-fired pizza, panini, homemade gelati, beer and wine. Coco makes artisan breads and fine pastries.

Chequamegon Book & Coffee, at 2 E. Bayfield St., is open daily.

Lake Superior Tap House, operated by Ashland's South Shore Brewery, is open weekends.

Paddling: Houghton Point from Memorial Park,

Dog-Paddle Kayaks rents sit-on kayaks and stand-up paddleboards and also offers guided tours, 715-373-2675.

Nightlife: StageNorth hosts play, concerts, dance performances and films, 715-373-1194. In summer, the professional Sidekick Theatre from the Twin Cities presents popular comedies, dramas and musicals.

Big Rock Campground is located three miles northwest of Washburn via County Hwy C and Big Rock Road. Known for its rustic campsites and a Class A trout stream, the adventurer will love exploring its possibilities. For more information on Big Rock Campground, please contact Bayfield County Tourism at 715-373-6125. (recommended)
