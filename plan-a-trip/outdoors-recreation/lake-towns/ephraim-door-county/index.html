Best things to do and see in Ephraim in Wisconsin's Door County

dd

Some tourists don't notice that Door County is made up of a series of towns. It can be hard to tell, especially on the Green Bay side of the peninsula, because of the non-stop stream of ice-cream stands, wineries, gift shops, bistros and inns.

But each town has a different personality, and each has attractions that set it apart from the others.

So . . . what makes Ephraim special?

Horseshoe Island. This rustic hiking trail encircles the entire island, which is found two miles off Peninsula State Park. To reach it, ambitious hikers typically kayak or boat from Nicolet Bay Beach.

Trip Tips: Ephraim

Getting there: It's 73 miles from Green Bay, sandwiched between Fish Creek and Sister Bay.

2016 events: June 18, Fyr Bal Festival. Oct. 8-9, Townline Art Fair.

Places to stay: High Point Inn, Evergreen Beach Resort, Pioneer Lane

Village Green Lodge. This quiet two-story inn, half a block from the waterfront, has a nice outdoor pool and WiFi. Owner Sue Sherman is renowned for her hot breakfasts, which are served buffet-style. Some rooms overlook the pool, and others the woods.

Ephraim Motel. This quiet, well-kept two-story motel on the hill between Ephraim and Sister Bay has an outdoor pool, grills and a playground on its roomy grounds.

Bay Breeze Resort. This two-story resort just south of downtown is across the road from the town beach and also has its own small beach with log swing.

Rooms face an outdoor hot tub and pool. Some rooms have whirlpools and gas fireplaces, and there are also two rental homes.

(from TravelWI) Edgewater Resort. If you find yourself looking for a place to stay in Door County, look no further than the Edgewater Resort. With consistent rave reviews, this cottage getaway offers a stunning view of Lake Michigan. For the past one hundred years, the Edgewater resort has been satisfying their patrons and beckoning a prompt return.
