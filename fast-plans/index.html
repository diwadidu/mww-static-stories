Fast Plans / Where to go now

  • Prime time on the Gunflint Trail

    On Minnesota's Gunflint Trail, this is prime time. Resist the urge to stay inside and wait for spring; head north now for the best cross-country skiing, snowshoeing and snowmobiling of the season.

    What to do: Ski 200 kilometers of superbly groomed trails into April. Snowshoe into the Boundary Waters and on trails.

    Details: For more, see Winter on Minnesota's Gunflint Trail.

  • Piles of snow in the Porkies

    On Michigan's Upper Peninsula, along Lake Superior, Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park has lavish piles of lovely snow when the rest of the region has been reduced to slush.

    What to do: Ski downhill on 42 runs, ski cross-country on 42 kilometers of trails or snowshoe anywhere.

    Events to catch: Feb. 26, Snowburst Winter Carnival with candlelight ski/snowshoe and fireworks.

    Where to stay: A yurt or the handsome Kaug Wudjoo Lodge on Union Bay.

    Details: For more, see White gold in the Porkies.

  • Party in St. Paul

    In Minnesota's capital, the biggest festival of the year comes in the depths of winter, started in 1886 in response to a New York City reporter's gibe that St. Paul was "the Siberia of North America.''

    Winter also is a good time to visit a museum or catch a play or concert in the compact downtown.

    What to do: See a show at the Ordway Center on Rice Park, the Fitzgerald Theater or the History Theatre. Visit the Science Museum of Minnesota or Minnesota History Center.

    Events to catch: Through Feb. 6, Winter Carnival.

    Details: For more, see Sightseeing in St. Paul.

  • Goofy winter fun in Minneapolis

    There's a lot going on in Minneapolis the next few weeks, thanks to a bunch of creative folks and sporty types who make it fun to be outdoors.

    What to do: Ski in Wirth Park. Snowshoe on Minnehaha Creek.

    Events to catch: Jan, 29, Art Sled Rally in Powderhorn Park. Jan. 29-30 and Feb. 5-6, Art Shanty Projects on Lake Harriet. Feb. 5-6, City of Lakes Loppet.

    Details: For more, see Chain of Lakes in Minneapolis.

  • Dubuque's views

    People who think Iowa is flat are in for a surprise when they visit Dubuque, a Mississippi port built on seven hills.

    What to do: Visit the National Mississippi River Museum & Aquarium. Hike or snowshoe in the Mines of Spain Recreation Area. Ski downhill at nearby Sundown Mountain.

    Events to catch: Jan. 15-16, Ice Fest at the River Museum.

    Details: See Destination Dubuque.

  • Cocooning in a cozy cabin

    Now that winter is deepening, many people are fantasizing about burrowing into a cozy cabin with a good book and a glass of wine. Here's how to find the perfect place to do some serious cocooning.

    Budget: Stay in a camper cabin or state-park guesthouse. See A roof in the woods and Lodgings in Minnesota state parks.

    Higher-range: Stay in someone else's cabin. See Renting a vacation house.

    More: See 20 perfect cabins and What to bring to a rented cabin.

  • Escape to Stillwater

    On the St. Croix River, Stillwater always has held a prominent place in Minnesota — first as a lumber town, and now as a favorite weekend destination.

    What to do: Ride the Brown's Creek State Trail from downtown. Shop on Main Street, especially for antiques. Take a trolley tour. Have a picnic in Pioneer Park overlooking the river valley. Sample wine at Northern Vineyards and beer at Maple Island Brewing.

    Events to catch: May 3-4, St. Croix Valley ArtOpener Studio Tour. May 10-12, Pottery Studio Tour.

    Details: For more, see Excursion to Stillwater.
