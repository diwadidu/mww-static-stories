<Saturday, December 10, 2022>

  1. Bentleyville Tour of Lights

    Duluth, MNNov. 19–Dec. 26

    Tour the retro-cool lighted decorations in Bayfront Festival Park, including a tree that dances to music, Dinosaurland and Penguin Park, and visit with Santa and Mrs. Claus. There's free hot chocolate, cookies, popcorn and marshmallows to roast over fire pits. Bring cash or food to donate to food shelves.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends Duluth story

  2. Holiday Fantasy in Lights

    Madison, WINov. 12–Jan. 1

    The free display in Olin Park is open from dusk to dawn.

    1. Event website

  3. Holiday Lights Festival

    Milwaukee, WINov. 17–Jan. 1

    The season-long lights festival includes more than 500,000 lights throughout downtown's parks and streets.

    1. MidwestWeekends story
    2. Event website

  4. Christkindlmarket

    Chicago, ILNov. 18–Dec. 24

    This is the most authentic Old World Christmas market, with many German vendors who bring over toys, ornaments and sweets. There's a Children's Lantern Parade, appearances by the Christkind and entertainment most days. In Daley Plaza in the Loop.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  5. Christkindlmarket Wrigleyville

    Chicago, ILNov. 18–Dec. 24

    This North Side satellite of the Daley Plaza Christkindlmarket features ice-skating, wreath-making, music and Santa's Workshop as well as holiday vendors.

    1. Event website

  6. ZooLights

    Chicago, ILDec. 2–14

    Admission to the annual display of 2.5 million lights at the Lincoln Park Zoo is $5 per person. Tickets are free on Mondays. Reserve time slots in advance.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  7. Holiday Kerstmarkt

    Holland, MIDec. 9–10

    This open-air European Christmas Market at the Eighth Street Market Place includes 18 timber booths offering Dutch imports, folk art, hand-knit apparel and other gifts.

    1. Event website

  8. Rotary Lights

    La Crosse, WINov. 25–Dec. 31

    In Riverside Park, see a holiday display of 3 million lights with music. It opens with a parade, and Santa and reindeer are there every night through Dec. 23, with a live Nativity some nights. There are also carriage and helicopter rides, an ice rink with free use of skates, and free s'mores at fire pits.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  9. European Christmas Market

    St. Paul, MNDec. 9–11

    This Christkindlmarkt in Union Depot features choirs, dancers, hot mulled wine, holiday beers, European-inspired food and artisan goods.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  10. Chris Kringle Market

    Ottawa, ILDec. 9–11

    This town on the Illinois River kicks off its downtown festival with a parade, followed by fireworks.

    1. Event website

  11. Door County Christkindlmarkt

    Sister Bay, WIDec. 9–11

    This market on the grounds of the Corner of the Past Museum, a recreated pioneer homestead south of town, features local vendors and artisans, live music, and German dishes served inside a large barn.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends Sister Bay story

  12. Lakeside Park Holiday Lights

    Fond du Lac, WINov. 24–Dec. 31

    This free light show on Lake Winnebago includes animated scenes and trees that dance to 20 minutes of music played on 87.9 FM (you can listen on your car radio).

    1. Event website

  13. Holidazzle

    Minneapolis, MNDec. 9–11

    This free holiday market in Loring Park, on the edge of downtown, features an artisan market, appearances by Santa and the illuminated Holidazzle Yeti, outdoor yoga, choirs, a beer garden and fireworks.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  14. Night of the Luminaria

    Galena, ILDec. 10

    More than 5,000 candle-lit luminaria line the streets of this historic town and 30 windows become animated.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends Galena story

  15. Home for the Holidays

    Eagle, WIDec. 10–11

    At this fun event at Old World Wisconsin, a complex of pioneer homesteads next to Kettle Moraine State Forest west of Milwaukee, you'll meet Father Christmas (and possibly Krampus), sip wassail, listen to brass carols and take a horse-drawn wagon ride.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story
