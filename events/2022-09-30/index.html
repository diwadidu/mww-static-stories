  1. Grundy County Corn Festival

    Morris, ILSept. 28–Oct. 2

    The festival in this town on the Illinois River between Ottawa and Joliet offers stagecoach and train rides, a crackerbox derby, a pie-eating contest, music, a carnival, Saturday fireworks and a parade at 2 p.m. Sunday.

    1. Event website

  2. World Music Festival

    Chicago, ILSept. 30–Oct. 9

    More than 80 artists perform at more than 30 venues.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  3. Oktoberfest

    La Crosse, WISept. 29–Oct. 1

    At this big riverfront festival, there's food, music and carnival rides, plus the opening Torchlight Parade and the Maple Leaf Parade on Saturday morning.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends La Crosse story

  4. Oktoberfest

    Amana, IASept. 30–Oct. 2

    There's German music and food at this Amana Colonies village, plus contests that include the Eisenmann, or Iron Man, competition: Teams roll kegs, walk a balance beam with full steins of beer and saw logs. The parade is at 10 a.m. Saturday.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  5. Northeast Iowa Artists' Studio Tour

    Decorah area, IASept. 30–Oct. 2

    Drive through scenic bluff country to see 42 artists at 31 sites around Decorah, Burr Oak, St. Lucas, Waukon, West Union, Cresco, New Albin, Lansing, Elgin and Clermont.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  6. Door County Fall Lighthouse Festival

    Sturgeon Bay, WISept. 30–Oct. 2

    The Door County Maritime Museum sponsors tours by air, land and water to lighthouses on the peninsula, some of which can be visited only during festival weekends. Reserve as early as possible.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  7. Iowa City Book Festival

    Iowa City, IASept. 28–Oct. 13

    The first North American town to be named a UNESCO City of Literature offers talks, readings, panels, exhibits and a walking tour of literary Iowa City.

    1. Event website
