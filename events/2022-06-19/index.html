<Sunday, June 19, 2022>

  1. Steamboat Days

    Winona, MNJune 15–19

    The main event is the 12:31 p.m. Sunday parade, one of the region's largest. There's also a carnival, craft show, music, power-boat races, a car show and fireworks.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  2. Bridgefest

    Houghton/Hancock, MIJune 16–19

    There will be helicopter rides, a water-ski show, a Friday parade and Saturday fireworks. The two towns are linked by the Keweenaw Peninsula's massive Portage Lift Bridge.

    1. Event website

  3. Great Galena Balloon Race

    Galena, ILJune 17–19

    Eagle Ridge Resort hosts two balloon races, two night glows and rides for the public. There's also live music, inflatables for kids, a Saturday art fair and a classic car show Saturday night.

    1. Event website
    2. Outdoors in Galena story

  4. Wooden Boat Show and Summer Solstice Festival

    Grand Marais, MNJune 17–19

    The harbor-side North House Folk School on Lake Superior kicks off summer with a boat parade, dancing, craft demonstrations, mini-courses and a solstice parade.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends Grand Marais story

  5. Stone Arch Bridge Festival

    Minneapolis, MNJune 18–19

    On the banks of the Mississippi, 200 artists show their wares. There will also be music, art for kids and a car show. The fair is not on the Stone Arch Bridge but on the west side of the river between Portland and North Fourth Avenue.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  6. Swedish Day

    Geneva, ILJune 19

    This heritage festival in Good Templar Park includes Maypole dancing, Viking reenactors, children's crafts, food and a Swedish market.

    1. Event website

  7. Jaycee Water Carnival

    Hutchinson, MNJune 13–19

    This town just west of Minneapolis, on the Crow River, celebrates with a boat parade, fireworks, human foosball tournament, canoe races, duck races, a car show and parade Sunday.

    1. Event website

  8. Rose Festival

    State Center, IAJune 16–19

    This town east of Ames calls itself Iowa's rose capital, and it celebrates with garden tours, children's games, a truck and tractor pull, music, fireworks and a parade at 10 a.m. Saturday.

    1. Event website

  9. Grant Park Music Festival

    Chicago, ILJune 15–Aug. 20

    Professional musicians, many of them world-famous, offer free concerts nearly every night in Millennium Park's beautiful Pritzker Pavilion. This year's schedule includes favorites by Beethoven, Mozart and Schubert as well as gospel, mariachi, choral, circus and Broadway evenings.

    1. Event website
