<Saturday, July 9, 2022>

  1. National Cherry Festival

    Traverse City, MIJuly 2–9

    This town on Lake Michigan attracts half a million people to events that include air shows, an arts fair, music, races, the big Cherry Royale Parade and many contests, including pie-eating and pit-spitting.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends Traverse City story

  2. Karl Oskar Days

    Lindstrom, MNJuly 6–10

    The festival in this Swedish town just north of the Twin Cities features fireworks, a car show, children's activities and a parade.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  3. Winnipeg Folk Festival

    Winnipeg, MBJuly 7–10

    This easy-going, family-friendly festival features more than 70 performers from eight countries, food and hand-made art from around the world plus a fun play area for children. 

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends Winnipeg story

  4. Heart of the North Rodeo

    Spooner, WIJuly 7–9

    Look for the four R's in this western Wisconsin town: roping, bull riding, steer wrestling and barrel racing. A parade is on Saturday.

    1. Event website

  5. Summerfest

    Milwaukee, WIJuly 7–9

    They call this the world's largest music festival. More than 800 acts perform on 12 stages along Lake Michigan. 

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends Milwaukee stories

  6. Iola Car Show

    Iola, WIJuly 7–9

    This show and swap meet brings up to 130,000 people to this village east of Stevens Point.

    1. Event website

  7. Hodag Country Festival

    Rhinelander, WIJuly 7–10

    This year, performers at this big fest include Neal McCoy, Jon Pardi, Kip Moore and Sam Hunt. There's a band contest, too.

    1. Event website

  8. Laura Ingalls Wilder pageant

    Walnut Grove, MNJuly 8–9

    The festival in this western Minnesota town revolves around the book "On the Banks of Plum Creek.'' 

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  9. Taste of Chicago

    Chicago, ILJuly 8–10

    This year, the city offers a "bite-size'' version of its big food and music festival in Grant Park, plus three pop-up festivals in neighborhoods on the last three Saturdays in June.

    1. Event website

  10. Northwest Water Carnival

    Detroit Lakes, MNJuly 8–17

    This big festival celebrates its 86th year with a sand-castle contest, water fights, ship-building (in less than 30 minutes), beer tasting, watermelon feed, bike tour and more. On the last day, there's a big parade at 1 p.m.

    1. Event website

  11. 80/35 Music Festival

    Des Moines, IAJuly 8–9

    This indie and jam music festival (named for the junction of two interstates) is at Western Gateway Park.

    1. Event website

  12. Blissfest

    Cross Village, MIJuly 8–10

    This family-friendly folk and roots festival on a farm includes reggae, country, zydeco, jazz and Celtic music on three stages and four workshop areas. There's also a juried arts and crafts fair. Rustic camping is available.

    1. Event website

  13. Bigfoot Days

    Remer, MNJuly 8–9

    This town in Chippewa National Forest, between Walker and Grand Rapids, calls itself the Home of Bigfoot. There will be a Bigfoot Olympics, a Bigfoot-calling contest, Bigfoot storytelling, a scavenger hunt and a petting zoo.

    1. Event website

  14. Taste and Glow Balloon Fest

    Wausau, WIJuly 8–10

    This festival features hot-air balloons and also music, kids' activities, craft and flea markets and sampling at Taste of Marathon County.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

  15. Phelps Mill Festival

    Phelps, MNJuly 9–10

    There's a juried arts and crafts fair, children's activities and music in this bucolic, tucked-away park along the Otter Tail River, next to an 1889 brick and clapboard mill that's open for tours.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends story

    Madison, WIJuly 9–10

    Nearly 500 artists from around the nation will be selling on Capitol Square and 140 Wisconsin artists around Monona Terrace, attracting more than 200,000 people.

    2. MidwestWeekends Madison stories

    2. MidwestWeekends story

    Chicago, ILJuly 8–10

  20. Wild Rice Festival

    Deer River, MNJuly 8–10

    This northern town near Grand Rapids offers a powwow Friday, wild-rice lunch, bike rodeo, sawdust scramble, singing competition and fireworks Saturday and a parade Sunday.

    1. Event website

  21. Fiesta Days

    McHenry, ILJuly 8–10

    There's a Music Fest, carnival, beach bash, car show and fireworks in Petersen Park the first weekend. The second weekend, there's an art and street fair Saturday and a 1:30 p.m. Sunday parade on Main Street.

    1. Event website

  22. Grant Park Music Festival

    Chicago, ILJune 15–Aug. 20

    Professional musicians, many of them world-famous, offer free concerts nearly every night in Millennium Park's beautiful Pritzker Pavilion. This year's schedule includes favorites by Beethoven, Mozart and Schubert as well as gospel, mariachi, choral, circus and Broadway evenings.

    1. Event website
    2. MidwestWeekends stories
